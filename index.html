Research

My research interests center on developing machine intelligence systems capable of autonomous and self-corrective decision-making through reinforcement learning. Below are foundational works I have contributed to across finance, telecommunications, multi-agent systems, and spiking neural networks using classical and quantum machine learning approaches.

1
Resource Allocation Optimization in 5G Networks using Variational Quantum Regressor
Param Pathak, Vidhi Oad, Aditya Prajapati, Nouhaila Innan
IEEE QCNC, 2024
paper

Quantum Machine Learning (QML) is a powerful tool for addressing complex telecommunications challenges. Our research provides a roadmap for integrating QML into the resource allocation framework, potentially improving the efficiency and performance of 5G networks through the application of Variational Quantum Regressor (VQR).

2
KASPER: Kolmogorov Arnold Networks for Stock Prediction and Explainable Regimes
Vidhi Oad, Param Pathak, Nouhaila Innan, Muhammad Shafique
Submitted to TMLR, 2025
arXiv

We introduce Kolmogorov-Arnold networks for stock prediction and explainable regimes (KASPER), a novel framework that integrates regime detection, sparse spline-based function modeling, and symbolic rule extraction. The framework identifies hidden market conditions and enables regime-specific forecasting while maintaining interpretability through symbolic learning.

3
Quantum-Enhanced Decision-Making in ACT-R: Cognitive Architectures, Decision-Making, and Quantum Optimization
Param Pathak, Nouhaila Innan, Alberto Marchisio, Muhammad Shafique
Quantum Computational AI (Book Chapter), Elsevier, 2026
book chapter

We explore integrating quantum optimization algorithms into cognitive architectures to enhance artificial intelligence (AI) systems. This chapter proposes a novel theoretical approach which embeds the quantum approximate optimization algorithm (QAOA) within the ACT-R cognitive architecture to improve decision-making under uncertainty.

4
The Evolution of IBM's Quantum Information Software Kit (Qiskit): A Review of its Applications
Param Pathak, K Tarakeshwar, Syed Sufiyan Ali, Shalini Devendrababu, Adarsh Ganesan
arXiv preprint, 2025
arXiv

This survey provides a systematic review of how Qiskit has evolved and what it has contributed to several critical fields including cryptography and cybersecurity, image and signal processing, climate science and energy applications, and finance. We explore several key applications and provide practical insights into their implementation.

5
DRiVE: Dynamic Recognition in VEhicles using snnTorch
Heerak Vora, Param Pathak, Parul Bakaraniya
IEEE ASSIC, 2025
paper

Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) mimic biological brain activity, processing data efficiently through an event-driven design. We introduce DRiVE, a vehicle detection model that uses spiking neuron dynamics to classify images, achieving 94.8% accuracy. These results highlight DRiVE's ability to distinguish vehicle classes effectively.
